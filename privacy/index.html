Map Marker privacy policy

What this policy applies to

This privacy policy applies to all 3 versions of the Map Marker app: the Android app, the iOS app and the web app.

Introduction of this privacy policy

This privacy policy explains how Map Marker can access, use, store, or share your personal data. In short, Map Marker never accesses, uses or stores your personal data outside of your device or your cloud account. Some sharing features of Map Marker allow you to explicitly share some data outside of your device or cloud account, but except that explicit manual sharing process initiated by yourself, Map Marker never shares your data outside of your device or cloud account.

Definition of personal data

The personal data collected by Map Marker falls under 3 categories:

  1. User geographic data: the list of geographic objects you can enter into the app which can contain geographic coordinates as well as a title, a description, a phone number, a color and associated images or media.
  2. User cloud account data: when explicitly granted access by you, Map Marker can have access to the your cloud account data, such as your email address, Google Drive files, or Dropbox files.
  3. User search queries: the search queries entered by you when searching for places with a third party search engine.
This privacy policy page will focus on user geographic data and user cloud account data only. When using the third party places search engines in Map Marker, depending on the search source you're using, you will need to refer to different privacy policies:
  • Here Search API: https://legal.here.com/en-gb/privacy
  • Google Places Search API: https://policies.google.com/privacy
  • Microsoft Bing Places search: https://privacy.microsoft.com/en-ca/privacystatement

How Map Marker accesses your data

The data is collected by Map Marker when you input the data manually or import the data as files or though other apps sharing features into Map Marker. When you grant Map Marker access to your cloud account data, Map Marker will use that access by reading from or writing to your cloud account data.

How Map Marker uses your data

Map Marker uses the data locally on the device with the different Map Marker features (displaying the markers list, displaying markers on the map, etc.). When you grant Map Marker access to your cloud account data, Map Marker will use that access to search for existing map files created by you or shared by others with you. When syncing map data, Map Marker will use the access to your cloud account data to download it on your device in order to be able to display it in the app.

How Map Marker stores your data

Your data is stored by Map Marker locally on the device, or into your cloud account data if you choose to sync your data into your cloud account.

Where your data lives

Your data lives on the device and on any cloud platform you explicitly configured Map Marker to sync to.

How Map Marker keeps your data

The data is kept on the device or on the cloud platforms as long as you don't delete it manually.

How Map Marker share your data

Map Marker does not share your data externally unless you perform explicit actions to share or sync your data. In the case of you manually sharing data from Map Marker, the exported data could be sent to any app on the device you choose to share with. In the case of sync, Map Marker allows you to configure an automated sync with some cloud storage platforms: your data is then shared by Map Marker with those cloud storage platforms.

What cloud storage platforms Map Marker can use

Currently Google Drive or Dropbox. When you grant access to Map Marker to a cloud platform, that access is used solely to read/write geographic synced data between the cloud platform and your device, and for nothing else.
